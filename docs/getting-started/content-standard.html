Localisation Guidelines

This guide outlines the conventions for localizing text within DIGIT to English, ensuring consistency and clarity across all products and interfaces.

Casing

Button Text & CTAs

Title Case

Capitalize the first letter of every word, except articles and conjunctions.

"Submit Feedback" "Create Account" "Learn More"

Body Text

Sentence Case

Capitalize only the first letter. Applies to help text, descriptions, error messages, validations, and links. Proper nouns are exceptions.

"Please enter a valid email address" "Contact support for assistance"

Headings, Sub-headings & Captions

Sentence Case

Default to Sentence case. Exceptions allowed when capitalizing words for emphasis.

Default

"Latest updates"

With emphasis

"Discover the New Features"

Additional Considerations

Consistency

Maintain uniform text formatting throughout the interface to enhance user understanding and reduce cognitive load.

Clarity

Ensure localized text is clear, concise, and easy to understand for the target audience, regardless of language or context.

Testing

Thoroughly test all localized text to verify accuracy and appropriateness within the context of the interface before shipping.

UX Copy Guidelines

Standards for voice, structure, and word choice across every product. Use alongside the Localisation Guide, which governs casing.

Core Principles

Clear over clever

Plain words beat polished ones. Write to be understood, not to impress.

Short over long

Cut every word that doesn't earn its place.

Active over passive

Say who does what. Active voice is shorter and clearer.

Consistent over creative

Use the same word for the same thing, everywhere.

User-first

Speak to the user, not about the system. Every word should serve them.

Voice and Tone

Voice stays the same

Direct, helpful, respectful

We do not lecture, joke, or apologise unless something has gone wrong.

Confirmation

Warm

Confirm what happened in one clear line. Skip the exclamation mark.

Error

Calm and specific

Say what went wrong and what to do next. Never blame the user.

Destructive action

Firm

Be clear about consequences. Don't soften language that needs to be direct.

Active and Passive Voice

Use active voice by default. If the user is acting, write actively. If the system did something and blame would be unhelpful, passive is fine.

Context Voice Example
Default for all UI copy Active "Save your changes" not "Changes should be saved."
System actions the user didn't trigger Passive OK "Your account was created on 12 May."
Errors caused by the system Passive OK "The file could not be uploaded."
Errors the user can fix Active "Enter a valid email address."

Element Guidelines

Headings

  • • Tell the user what the screen or section is about. Skip filler like "Welcome to".
  • • Keep under 6 words where possible. Sentence case. No full stops.
  • • Front-load the keyword the most important word comes first.
✓ Do ✕ Don't
Payment methodsManage your payment methods here
Account settingsWelcome to the account settings page
Pending approvalsApprovals that are currently pending

Descriptions & Supporting Text

  • • One sentence is usually enough. Two is the ceiling.
  • • Explain what the section does. Do not repeat the heading. End with a full stop.
  • • Avoid "please" unless asking for something inconvenient.
✓ Do ✕ Don't
Add the cards you use to pay for orders.Here you can add the cards that you would like to use for paying for your orders.
Choose how you want to receive updates.Please kindly select your preferred notification preferences from the options below.

Labels

  • • Use a noun or short noun phrase never a sentence.
  • • Sentence case. No colon, no full stop.
  • • Match the data, not the field type. "Email address" beats "Enter your email."
✓ Do ✕ Don't
Email addressPlease enter your email:
Phone numberMobile no.*
Date of birthDOB

Calls to Action (CTAs)

  • • Start with a verb. Title Case. No full stop. Keep to 1–3 words.
  • • Be specific "Save Draft" beats "Submit". "Delete Account" beats "OK".
  • • Pair opposites: primary moves forward, secondary cancels or goes back.
✓ Do ✕ Don't
Create AccountSubmit
Delete PropertyYes
Save Draft  /  DiscardOK  /  Cancel
Add Payment MethodClick Here

Error Messages

  • • Say what went wrong and what to do next.
  • • Do not blame the user. Avoid the word "invalid" where possible.
  • • Be specific about the field or action.
✓ Do ✕ Don't
Enter a valid email address.Invalid input.
Password must be at least 8 characters.Wrong password format.
We couldn't save your changes. Try again.Error occurred.

Empty States & Confirmations

  • • Empty state: tell the user what will appear there, and how to add the first item.
  • • Confirmation: one line, what happened. No exclamation mark.
✓ Do ✕ Don't
No properties yet. Add one to get started.Nothing to show here!
Application submitted.Success! Your application has been submitted successfully!

Word Choice

Pick one term per concept and use it everywhere. Never switch between "property", "asset", "unit", and "listing" for the same thing.

Numbers, Dates & Punctuation

Numbers

Always use numerals in UI "3 items", not "three items".

Dates

12 May 2026 (long) or 12/05/2026 (short). Pick one format per surface.

Time

24-hour for government / back-office; 12-hour with am/pm for citizens.

Punctuation

Oxford comma in lists of 3+. No exclamation marks. No emojis in core UI.

Accessibility & Inclusion

Do

Use meaningful link text View invoice so screen reader users understand where each link goes without surrounding context.

Use gender-neutral language "they" works for everyone and removes assumptions about the reader.

Don't

Rely on directional cues alone "the button below" breaks on reflowed mobile layouts. Name the button instead.

Use idioms, sports metaphors, or humour they often fail to translate across languages and cultures.

Rely on colour alone in copy. If you say "the red items", also name them for users who cannot perceive colour.

Pre-ship Checklist

Before any screen ships, the copy should pass each of these.

  • Heading under 6 words

    Sentence case, no full stop. The heading tells the user what the screen is about.

  • Description is one or two sentences

    Does not repeat the heading. Ends with a full stop.

  • Labels are nouns, sentence case

    Consistent with labels used elsewhere for the same data across the product.

  • CTAs start with a verb, Title Case

    Say what will happen when the user taps it. "Save Draft" beats "Submit".

  • Voice is active

    Unless the system, not the user, did the action then passive is acceptable.

  • No "please", exclamation marks, or "click here"

    Also avoid "invalid" describe what went wrong instead.

  • Same term for the same concept

    Used consistently across the screen and the entire flow. Never switch synonyms mid-journey.

  • Error messages tell the user what to do next

    Specific, actionable, and blame-free. "Enter a valid email" beats "Invalid input".
